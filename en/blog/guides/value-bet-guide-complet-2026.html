📖 Definition of Value Bet

A value bet exists when the real probability of an event is higher than the implicit probability of the odds offered by the bookmaker.

In other words: the bookmaker has underestimated the chances of an outcome. You have a mathematical advantage over him. It's exactly like a casino making an error on its probabilities — if you detect it, you play with an advantage.

Simple formula: Value = (Estimated Probability × Odds) − 1
If the result is positive → value bet (opportunity) | If negative → no value

🔢 Concrete Example

Situation: Arsenal plays against a mid-table club. Our AI estimates Arsenal has 70% chance to win. Betclic offers odds of 1.60 for Arsenal's victory.

Calculation: (0.70 × 1.60) − 1 = +12% value

This means that on 100 identical bets, you win on average €12 for every €100 wagered. This is an excellent value bet according to our AI.

💡
Implicit odds: To convert odds to probability → 1 ÷ Odds. Example: odds 1.60 → 1÷1.60 = 62.5% implicit probability. If you think the true probability is 70%, you have an edge of +7.5%.

📊 Different Types of Value Bets

Simple Value Bet

The most direct bet — win, draw or loss. Our AI finds edges on outcomes when bookmakers overestimate or underestimate a team following a misleading recent result.

Value Bet BTTS (Both Teams To Score)

Bookmakers often miscalculate BTTS probabilities on mid-table matches. Our AI detects regular edges on this market, notably in Premier League and Bundesliga.

Value Bet Over/Under

Total goals is a market where anomalies are frequent. A match between two attacking teams in form facing two fragile defenses can offer excellent edge on Over 2.5.

Long-Term Value Bet

Bets on the winner of a championship or competition. Bookmakers make more mistakes over the long term because probabilities change throughout the season.

💰 Kelly Criterion — Optimal Stake

Once the value bet is identified, the question is: how much to bet? Kelly's criterion gives the mathematically optimal answer.

Kelly's formula: Stake (%) = (Edge) ÷ (Odds − 1)
Example: Edge +12%, Odds 1.60 → Stake = 0.12 ÷ 0.60 = 20% of bankroll
In practice: use fractional Kelly (1/4 of Kelly) to limit risk = 5% here.

Our AI automatically calculates the optimal stake for each value bet and displays a recommendation between 1% and 5% of the bankroll.

❌ Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️
Mistake #1: Confusing value bet with safe bet. A value bet at 3.00 odds can lose 7 times out of 10 and still be profitable long-term. Consistency beats short-term results.

Mistake #2: Betting too much. Even with a +20% edge, betting more than 5% of the bankroll exposes you to ruin in case of a normal losing streak.

Mistake #3: Choosing only big favorites. Very low odds (1.10-1.25) rarely offer value — bookmakers are accurate on these markets.
