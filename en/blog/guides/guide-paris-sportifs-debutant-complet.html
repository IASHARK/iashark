💰 Rule #1 — The Bankroll

Before placing a single bet, define your bankroll — the total budget you're willing to dedicate to betting. This is money you accept to lose entirely without impacting your life.

The golden rule: Never bet more than 2 to 5% of your bankroll per bet.
Bankroll €200 → max stake €4 to €10 per bet.
This rule protects you from inevitable losing streaks, even with a good strategy.

🎯 The 4 Types of Bets

1. Single Bet — The Classic

You bet on a single outcome. Clear, simple, recommended for beginners. Example: PSG win, Over 2.5 goals, BTTS Yes.

2. Accumulator Bet — Risky

You combine multiple selections. Odds multiply but all results must be correct. An accumulator of 5 selections with 65% success rate each has only 11% chance of winning. Our AI rarely recommends accumulators of more than 3 selections.

3. Live Betting — In-Play

You bet during the match. Odds change in real time. Requires good tactical knowledge and excellent emotional control.

4. Long-Term Bets

Championship winner, top scorer of the season, relegated team. High odds but capital locked up for long.

❌ The 7 Beginner Mistakes

⚠️
1. Betting with emotion — Never bet on your favorite team or against your rival. Emotion clouds judgment.

2. Chasing losses — Doubling your stake after a loss to "recover" is guaranteed ruin.

3. Ignoring odds — Even the best prediction can be a bad deal if the odds are too low.

4. Too many accumulators — Accumulators enrich bookmakers, not bettors.

5. Betting without strategy — Betting on instinct without analysis = losing over time.

6. Neglecting bankroll — Staking too much exposes you to ruin even with a good strategy.

7. One bookmaker only — Multiple accounts let you always take the best available odds.
